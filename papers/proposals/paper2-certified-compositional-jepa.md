# Proposal — A Predictability Certificate for Equivariant World Models

> **Codename: paper2** (the flagship; absorbs the former "paper3" structure-of-prediction idea as its time/resolution axis). Direction approved 2026-06-02; merged 2026-06-02.
> **One-line thesis**: *Scale buys interpolation without guarantees; structure buys a **certificate**.* For an equivariant latent world model we give a single **3-axis predictability certificate** — over **configuration** (the generated group $\langle S\rangle$), **horizon** ($T$), and **resolution** ($\epsilon$) — whose certified region is exactly the **symmetry-protected, coarse-invariant, low-composition** corner, with boundary set by the group $\langle S\rangle$ **and** the dynamics' Lyapunov spectrum. No scale-only model has such a certificate on **any** axis.

---

## 1. The breakthrough claim (impact = a new *kind* of result, not a benchmark number)

Scaling lowers test error on the data manifold but issues **no statement about a specific unseen situation**. We claim a different *kind* of result — a **certificate**: a provable, computable region of situations the model is guaranteed to handle, *without further training or data*. Crucially the certificate spans **three orthogonal axes at once**:

- **Configuration** $w\in\langle S\rangle$ — generalize across the exponentially-large monoid generated by $k$ primitive symmetries (verified on the $k$ generators).
- **Horizon** $T$ — how far into the future the guarantee reaches.
- **Resolution** $\epsilon$ — at what level of detail.

The master finding: the certified region is the **coarse-invariant, symmetry-protected, low-$|w|$ corner**, and its boundary is set by $\langle S\rangle$ + the Lyapunov spectrum $\{\lambda_j\}$. This reframes the Bitter-Lesson debate from a *degree* contest (who needs less data — scale wins eventually) to a *kind* contest (who can issue a certificate — only structure can). **Impact = a tool for the field**: a single criterion that says *what* an equivariant world model can certifiably predict — and, read across domains, *why* celestial mechanics is predictable for millennia, weather dies at two weeks, and long-range forecasting systems can only ever be coarse.

(Model of impact: a mechanism/theory paper à la JEPA, with a clean proof-of-principle at 1–2 GPU scale — **not** a scaled-SOTA benchmark. See §7.)

## 2. Setup and the master theorem (the three axes, unified)

**Setup.** Encoder $E:\mathcal X\to\mathcal Z$, action-conditioned predictor $f:\mathcal Z\times\mathcal A\to\mathcal Z$; a (mon­oid) element $g$ acts by $g\cdot x$ on states, by an **orthogonal** representation $\rho(g)$ on latents, by $\sigma(g)$ on actions. **Equivariance**: $E(g\cdot x)=\rho(g)E(x)$, $f(\rho(g)z,\sigma(g)a)=\rho(g)f(z,a)$. Let $f^{(T)}$ be the $T$-step rollout, $\mathrm{Err}_T(x;\vec a)=\lVert f^{(T)}(E(x),\vec a)-E(x^{\text{true}}_T)\rVert$ (or the closed-loop cost $J$).

**(Axis 1 — Configuration) Lemma (composition closure).** Equivariance on each generator $g_i\in S$ ⟹ equivariance on every word $w=g_{i_1}\cdots g_{i_m}\in\langle S\rangle$, since $\rho$ is a monoid homomorphism: $\rho(g_{i_1}\!\cdots g_{i_m})=\rho(g_{i_1})\cdots\rho(g_{i_m})$. *$k$ checks certify an exponential set.*

**Theorem A (exact certificate).** Under exact equivariance with orthogonal $\rho$: for every $w\in\langle S\rangle$, every $T$, every action sequence,
$$
\mathrm{Err}_T(w\cdot x;\sigma(w)\vec a)=\mathrm{Err}_T(x;\vec a),\qquad J(w\cdot x;w\cdot\text{goal})=J(x;\text{goal}).
$$
*Proof sketch.* $f^{(T)}$ equivariant by induction; orthogonality kills $\rho(w)$ inside the norm. (Multi-step, full-monoid lift of one-step [B].) $\square$

**(Axes 2+3 — Horizon × Resolution) Theorem B (spectral degradation — the load-bearing, honest result).** Promote the scalar Lipschitz $L$ to the predictor-Jacobian **spectrum** $\{e^{\lambda_j}\}$ along latent channels (e.g. the $\ell$-isotypic blocks). With measured residuals $\epsilon_{\max}$ (encoder, per generator) and $\delta$ (predictor),
$$
\big\lvert\mathrm{Err}_T(w\cdot x)-\mathrm{Err}_T(x)\big\rvert\ \le\ \sum_{\text{channels }j} c_j\,(m\,\epsilon_{\max}+T\,\delta)\,e^{\lambda_j T},\qquad m=|w|,
$$
so **channel $j$ is certified to horizon** $T_j(\epsilon)\sim \tfrac{1}{\lambda_j}\log\tfrac{1}{\epsilon}$ for $\lambda_j>0$, and $T_j=\infty$ for $\lambda_j\le 0$.

**Corollary (the certified region).** The set $\mathcal C=\{(w,T,\epsilon):|\Delta\mathrm{Err}|\le\epsilon\}$ is the **coarse-invariant, slow ($\lambda_j\le0$), low-$|w|$** corner; its boundary is $\langle S\rangle$ (config) $\times$ $\{\lambda_j\}$ (horizon/resolution). With **exact** equivariance ($\epsilon_{\max},\delta\sim10^{-6}$ by [A]) the config axis is unbounded and only the Lyapunov spectrum limits horizon×resolution — i.e. **a horizon × resolution trade-off**: $\text{horizon}\times\text{demanded-resolution}\lesssim$ const$(\{\lambda_j\})$. This is the formal content of "ultra-long forecasts must be coarse" (eclipses for millennia; weather at ~2 weeks; 推背图-style texts assert only regime-level change — structurally the *correct* move).

## 3. The open hinge (headline **measured conjecture**, not hidden)

Theorems A–B hold for *whatever* channels are slow; what makes the symmetry axis and the time axis the **same** structure is a Noether-type bridge:

> **Conjecture (group ⇒ slow).** When the symmetry is a symmetry of the **dynamics** (not merely the encoder), the group-invariant ($\ell{=}0$) channels coincide with the conserved/slow ($\lambda_j\le0$) channels. Hence the symmetry that gives across-orbit (configuration) generalization is the *same* structure that gives long-horizon (time) predictability.

This is **not automatic** (group-invariant $\ne$ dynamically-conserved in general) — it is the paper's central, **falsifiable** claim, and we treat it as a *measured* conjecture: train the model, estimate per-channel $\lambda_j$, and test whether the $\ell{=}0$ block is in fact the slow/conserved one. Stating it openly (rather than assuming it) is the paper's sharpest, most honest edge.

## 6. One experiment, three axes (the killer demo)

**World.** Object-centric 3D scene (Step 19/24): per-object $\mathrm{SE}(3)$ + object permutation $S_O$ → exponential $\langle S\rangle$. (Optional clean warm-up: the I Ching $\mathbb{Z}_2^6$ testbed for the config axis alone.)

**Certified model.** Exact-equivariant JEPA + equivariant planner; verify equivariance on the $k$ generators ([A]). Then, **with no further training**, read **one rollout dataset along all three axes**:
- **Config slice** (vary $|w|=1..M$): $\mathrm{Err}$ flat over $\langle S\rangle$ (certified) — overlay Thm-B bound.
- **Horizon slice** (vary $T=1..H$): per-channel decay; the $\ell{=}0$/coarse channels stay flat, fine channels decay $\sim e^{\lambda_j T}$.
- **Resolution slice** (vary $\epsilon$ / which irreps): the certified horizon $T_j(\epsilon)\sim\log(1/\epsilon)/\lambda_j$ trade-off, traced out.
- **Hinge test**: measure $\{\lambda_j\}$; check the $\ell{=}0$ block is the slow one (test §3).

**Headline plots.** (1) the certified region $\mathcal C$ in $(|w|,T,\epsilon)$; (2) **structure vs scale**: certified region (exponential, from $k$ generators) vs the region a **scaled non-equivariant baseline** + augmentation actually covers (polynomial in data) — and it carries **no certificate** on any axis.

**Discovery arm.** Repeat with $S$ discovered by an active-inference agent → earned certificate.

## 7. Honesty

- **Achievable (1–2 GPU):** the theorem(s) + a convincing small-scale proof-of-principle (the three slices + structure-vs-scale). Impact via the *idea + certificate + clean demo*.
- **Not claimed:** beating a scaled SOTA on a large real benchmark.
- **Genuine risks:** (a) real-world symmetry is approximate/weak → Theorem B *quantifies* the degradation and we *measure* it; if too weak, the honest conclusion is "certificate meaningful only above a symmetry-content threshold" (Step 22 boundary) — still a useful law. (b) the **group⇒slow hinge (§3) may fail** — that is the headline conjecture, reported as measured, not assumed.

### Attack points → contingencies
1. *"Just [B] restated."* → closure + multi-step + spectral stratification + the hinge are new; [B] is one corner.
2. *"Approximate symmetry kills it."* → the measured Theorem-B degradation (P4), not an unmodeled hole.
3. *"Toy / hand-wired group."* → discovery arm earns $S$; the I Ching testbed is a *clean* config-axis demo, the object world is the real one.
4. *"Hinge is hand-waved."* → it is stated as a falsifiable conjecture and **measured** ($\lambda_j$ of the $\ell{=}0$ block).
5. *"Compounding rollout error."* → measure $\{\lambda_j\}$; with exact equivariance + $\lambda_j\le0$ channels the bound is tight; report the horizon where it holds.
